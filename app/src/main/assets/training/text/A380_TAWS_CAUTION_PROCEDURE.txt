OBSTACLE AHEAD, TERRAIN AHEAD, TERRAIN TERRAIN, TOO LOW TERRAIN
During night or I.M.C. conditions:
Simultaneously:

PULL UP TOGA

AUTO PILOT....OFF
PITCH....PULL UP
Pull up to full backstick and mantain in that position

THRUST LEVERS....TOGA
SPEED BRAKE LEVER....CHECK RETRACTED
BANK....WINGS LEVEL or ADJUST
The aircraft obtains the best climb performance when the wings are as level as possible.The flight crew can adjust bank while climbing, provided that turning is the safest action. Note: For some airports, the operator may define a specific procedure.

DO NOT CHANGE CONFIGURATION (SLATS/FLEPS/GEAR) UNTIL CLEAR OF OBSTACLE.

During daylight and V.M.C. conditions,with terrain and obstacles clearly in sight:
FLIGHT PATH....ADJUST
Adjust pitch, bank and thrust to silence the alert.
Note: For some airports, the operator may define a specific procedure.

SINK RATE
Above 1 thousand feet A.A.L. I.M.C. or above 500 feet A.A.L. V.M.C.
FLIGHT PATH....ADJUST
Adjust pitch, bank and thrust to silence the alert.

Below 1 thousand feet A.A.L. IMC or below 500 feet A.A.L. V.M.C.
GO AROUND....CONSIDER

DON'T SINK
FLIGHT PATH....ADJUST
Adjust pitch, bank and thrust to silence the alert.

GLIDE SLOPE
Above 1 thousand feet A.A.L. I.M.C. or above 500 feet A.A.L. V.M.C.
FLIGHT PATH....ADJUST
Adjust pitch and thrust to reduce the vertical deviation from the glide slope.

when conditions require a deliberate approach below glideslope:
GLIDE SLOPE MODE....OFF
Below 1 thousand feet A.A.L. IMC or below 500 feet A.A.L. V.M.C.
GO AROUND....CONSIDER

TOO LOW GEAR, TOO LOW FLAPS
GO AROUND....PERFORM
