AIR DATA (A.D.R.s/STAND BY INSTRUMENTS) ....CROSS CHECK

Crosscheck all speeds and altitudes indications.

Alternate sources may be used to evaluate the air data:
- GPS altitude
- GPS and IRS Ground Speeds, taking into account altitude and wind effect.

The resulting speed computed by F.W.S. on ECAM can help the crew.

IF AT LEAST ONE AIR DATA RELIABLE:

RELIABLE AIR DATA ....USE
AT LEAST ONE A.D.R.:.... KEEP ON
UNRELIABLE A.D.R.s ....OFF

This prevents the flight control laws from using unreliable ADR data.

IF NO AIR DATA RELIABLE:

A.D.R. 1+2+3 PUSH BUTTONs ....OFF

All ADRs must be switched off to replace the P.F.D.’s normal speed scale and altitude
indication by the BUSS and GPS altitude indication.

SPEED ....FLY THE GREEN

Fly within the green area of the speed scale to ensure safe flight.

For slats/flaps retraction, it is recommended to fly at the top of the green area
of the speed scale.

Before extending the slats/flaps, it is recommended to fly at the bottom of the
speed scale green area, and to be in straight flight.

FLIGHT PATH VECTOR / VV AVAILABLE

In order to display the FLIGHT PATH VECTOR(VV), select TRACK-FLIGHT PATH ANGLE via the HEADING VERTICAL SPEED /TRACK FLIGHT PATH ANGLE push button on the A.F.S. CONTROL PANEL.

SPEED BRAKE: DO NOT USE

The BUSS is tuned for the aerodynamic model with the speed brake retracted.
The speed brake extension must be avoided as it would affect the relation between
ANGLE OF ATTACK and airspeed, therefore making the BUSS unreliable.
